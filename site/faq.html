Twenty questions we hear most often, with real answers. If yours isn't here, call us — we'll add it.

Pricing & what's included

Why are your prices higher than [competitor X]?

They probably aren't — once you compare apples to apples. Most competitor "starting at" prices are home only, no delivery, no setup, no skirting, no steps, no anything. Our prices include delivery within 50 miles, setup, skirting, and basic steps. Get their quote in writing with line items and call us — we'll show you the math.

What's actually included in the price I see?

Home from the factory, delivery (zone-based), setup on a pre-prepped site, skirting, basic steps, anchor tie-downs, and our service warranty for one year. Not included: site prep (foundation, utilities, septic, driveway), HVAC tonnage upsizing, and county permit fees. Site prep typically runs $8,000–$22,000 depending on lot.

Is the price negotiable?

Sometimes, on aging inventory or off-season. Not on new factory orders — those are at our cost plus a fixed margin we publish. We'd rather hold the price than cut it and make it up on hidden fees later.

Financing

What credit score do I need?

580 is the practical floor for most chattel loans. 620+ opens up better rates. Land+Home FHA loans need 580 with 3.5% down. Below 580, options exist but get expensive — we'll be straight about whether it makes sense before you apply.

Can I get an FHA loan on a manufactured home?

Yes — if you own (or are buying) the land it sits on, the home is post-1976 HUD-code, and it's permanently affixed. We do this regularly. Your home and land become one mortgage at conventional-style rates.

How much down payment do I need?

Chattel: typically 5–20%. FHA Land+Home: 3.5%. VA: 0% if you qualify. We'll match you to the program with the lowest down for your situation, not the highest commission.

Land & site prep

Can I put a manufactured home anywhere?

No. County zoning matters, neighborhood covenants matter, and some HOAs prohibit them outright. Most rural and many suburban SC counties allow them on lots ≥ 0.5 acre. Send us your address — we'll check the zoning and covenants for free before you commit.

Do I need a foundation?

Some kind, yes. SC requires either a permanent foundation (block piers + concrete footings) or an engineered system. FHA loans require permanent. The pier-and-skirt approach is cheapest at ~$4–6k; full perimeter wall is $14–22k.

What about water, septic, electric?

Your responsibility, but we coordinate the contractors. Septic is usually $6–12k depending on perc test. Well is $8–15k. Electric service drop is $1.5–4k. We get three bids and you pick.

Quality & longevity

How long do these homes last?

HUD-code homes (post-1976) routinely last 50+ years with normal maintenance. The pre-1976 "mobile home" reputation doesn't apply to anything we sell. Roof, HVAC, and water heater have the same lifespans as in a site-built home.

Are they hurricane-safe?

The homes we sell are built to HUD Wind Zone II (110 mph) standard, with Zone III (130+ mph) available for coastal counties. Anchored properly, they outperform many older site-built homes in Carolina storms. We follow SC's tie-down code on every install.

How do they hold value?

On owned land: similar to site-built, with land doing most of the appreciation. On rented land: they depreciate like vehicles, especially in the first 10 years. If long-term value matters, buy land too.

Process & timeline

How long from contract to move-in?

If you own land and the home is in our inventory: 8–14 weeks. If we're factory-ordering: add 6–10 weeks. Land+Home with site prep: 12–20 weeks. Weather and permits are the main variables.

Can I customize a home?

Inventory homes: limited — paint, flooring upgrades, appliance package. Factory orders: extensively. Layout, finishes, cabinets, windows, porch options. We'll walk you through the options sheet for whichever model you pick.

What's the warranty?

Manufacturer: 1 year bumper-to-bumper, 5–10 years structural depending on builder. Ours: 1 year on installation work (leveling, plumbing connections, electrical hookups). Beyond that, we still come out — we just charge for parts/labor.

The awkward ones

Aren't these "trailer parks"?

Some manufactured homes are placed in land-lease communities (formerly "trailer parks"); most aren't. The home itself is the same — what varies is whether you own the land. We mostly sell to people putting homes on their own land.

Will my neighbors hate me?

Modern multi-section homes with proper foundations and skirting are visually indistinguishable from modular or site-built ranch homes from the curb. We've placed dozens in established neighborhoods. The 1970s stigma doesn't match the 2020s product.

What happens if you go out of business?

The manufacturer warranty stays valid regardless. For service, any licensed installer in SC can work on your home. We've been operating since 1998 and are profitable, but it's a fair question — and the honest answer is your home isn't dependent on us being here.
