Three loan types.
One straightforward conversation.

We don't take a cut from lenders, so we can recommend whichever works best for your situation. Here's the plain-English breakdown.

Compare loan types

Which one fits you?

01
If you don't own land yet

Land + Home

One loan covers the home, the land, and the site work. Treated like a traditional mortgage — same paperwork, same closing process. Usually the best rate available.

Typical rate6.5–7.5%
Term15–30 yrs
Down3.5–10%
TypeReal estate
02
If you already own land

Chattel loan

Personal-property loan for the home only. Faster closing, less paperwork, but higher rates than real-estate loans. Most popular option for buyers placing the home on family land or a long-term lease.

Typical rate8.5–11%
Term15–25 yrs
Down5–15%
TypePersonal
03
If you qualify for FHA

FHA Title I & II

Government-backed program for manufactured homes. Title II loans behave like a mortgage when bought with land; Title I is home-only. Strict construction standards, but better rates if you qualify.

Typical rate6.5–8%
Term20–30 yrs
Down3.5%
TypeGovt-backed

Common questions

Financing, plainly explained.

What credit score do I need?

Realistically, 620 is the floor for a competitive rate. We work with lenders who'll go down to 580 — but expect rates 1.5–2 points higher. If you're rebuilding, ask about Cascade.

Can I finance a used manufactured home?

Yes — but it's harder, and usually only homes from 1976 or newer. Some lenders cap age at 20 years.

Why are chattel rates so much higher than mortgages?

Personal-property loans don't have the same federal backing as real estate, so lenders charge more for risk. The trade-off: faster, simpler closings.

How long does pre-approval take?

Same day for most of our partner lenders. Full underwriting and closing usually 30–45 days for Land+Home, 14–21 days for chattel.
